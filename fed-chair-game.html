🏛️ Fed Chair — The Dual Mandate Game

Q 0 / 40
🎯 Your job: each quarter, set the federal funds rate to steer inflation to your target (2%) and unemployment to ≈4%, then press Advance Quarter. Rate moves take 2–3 quarters to work. You may also announce a different inflation target (0–5%) in the policy panel below — but Congress grades you against 2%.
How to play

Each turn is one quarter. Set the federal funds rate, then advance. Your rate moves the real rate relative to the neutral rate (r*); a restrictive stance cools demand and an easy one stimulates it — but with a 2–3 quarter lag, so plan ahead. Demand moves unemployment (Okun's law) and inflation (Phillips curve). Keep inflation near 2% and unemployment near 4% for 40 quarters. Beware: if inflation strays far from your target for long, expectations de-anchor and it becomes much harder to bring down.

The neutral rate r* drifts unpredictably (shown next to the fed funds rate), and shocks — oil, demand swings, crises — hit often. A rate that's right today won't be right for long, so set-and-forget will slowly steer the economy off course. Watch the readouts each quarter and keep adjusting.

You may also announce your own inflation target (0–5%). Expectations anchor to whatever you announce, weighted by your credibility — a higher announced target makes disinflation less painful. But re-announcing costs credibility (markets punish moving goalposts), and Congress grades you against 2% regardless of what you announce. You're fired at 12% inflation, sustained deflation, or 12% unemployment.
